# Get Rich Quick Manual — Final

This manual contains original task guidance plus added supplemental guidance. Added guidance is placed next to the most relevant official passage and is meant to clarify practical application, especially for screenshot/OCR-based rating workflows.

## Source labels

- **Official guidance** = original task instructions and category guidance.
- **Added guidance** = supplemental guidance integrated into this manual to clarify practical application; it is part of this manual but not part of the original official guidance.
- **Non-manual indicators** = useful case-specific indicators not explicitly present anywhere in this manual; these may be mentioned in per-task output when relevant.

# Official guidance

N.B.: These guidelines were updated in December 2018. Please take some time to review them carefully.

In this project you are asked to review ad creatives or web pages, and to decide whether or not they fall under the Get Rich Quick category. The results of this work will allow publishers to exclude ads related to Get Rich Quick from their websites.

Note: If you have any ad blocking software enabled, please pause it for the duration of this task, or you will not be able to complete it.

## Task structure

Each task will contain one ad creative or one web page. Your task is to review this ad creative or web page and decide whether it falls under the Get Rich Quick category. You might get a lot of ad creative tasks or a lot of web page tasks in a row; this is normal and expected.

IMPORTANT: When labelling a web page, please take its URL into account. You will find the URL right underneath the web page. If there is evidence in the text of the URL itself that the content of the webpage belongs to the Get Rich Quick category, please rate accordingly. Example:

http://turboprosperity.com/tag/cash-generating-system/ (cash generating system)

### Added guidance — URL evidence

A visible webpage URL can be sufficient evidence when it clearly points to the Get Rich Quick category, even if the visible page or creative is sparse. Do not rely on the URL alone if its category implication is weak, generic, or contradicted by the visible content.

Some ad creatives and web pages might be unrateable. The most common reasons to select "unrateable" are:

- content is not primarily in your rating language
- content does not load
- content has no text, or contains too little content to understand what it is about
- content does not render well enough to interpret
- there's an error preventing you from viewing the content
- page contains little content and requires registering or logging in
- web page contains a video and lacks textual content that describes its contents
- web page has offensive content that you're not comfortable examining closely

Please leave a brief comment explaining why you think the ad creative or the web page is Unrateable.

Comments. If you have comments regarding particular tasks, please use the comment section for that.

Not sure how to rate. If you are not sure how to rate a whole task or part of it, please still provide your best answer and additionally add a brief comment saying that you are not sure. For example, "not sure how to rate" or "not sure how to rate creative since ...". It is very important that you leave comments in such situations. It will allow us to confirm the ratings and enrich this set of guidelines with additional explanations.

### Added guidance — screenshot and OCR evidence discipline

Use only visible evidence from the screenshot or OCR text: visible text, visible URL, imagery, product name, CTA, page state, and visible branding. Do not assume what happens after clicking. Do not infer hidden landing-page behavior. If evidence is unclear, mark it unclear rather than inventing details.

### Added guidance — Unrateable vs uncertain

- Unrateable means you cannot assess the content at all because it failed to load, is in the wrong language, is behind a login wall, has too little visible evidence, or otherwise cannot be interpreted.
- Uncertain means there is assessable content but classification is difficult; in that case, give your best rating and leave a brief comment.
- Do not use Unrateable as a fallback for ambiguous but assessable Get Rich Quick cases.
- If the official Get Rich Quick uncertainty rule applies, suspicious/scammy but uncertain assessable cases should be rated positive.

# Scope of the Get Rich Quick category

## Official guidance

The Get Rich Quick category includes:

✔️ offers that promise to share lucrative business secrets with consumers;
✔️ ads which offer significant money or merchandise for little or nothing in return;
✔️ multi-level marketing (sometimes known as "pyramid schemes", in which one buys goods and recruits others to sell them);
✔️ "get rich quick" schemes promising high earnings;
✔️ work from home offers;
✔️ apps that promise cash rewards for the time users spend on the apps [NEW]

Products often take the form of books, seminars, or other media claiming to contain business secrets; sometimes though it is impossible to understand what the product is based on the ad.

The Get Rich Quick category excludes:

❌ legitimate financial planning services;
❌ stock funds;
❌ banks;
❌ consumer real estate services;
❌ official lotteries and promotional sweepstakes;
❌ offers of small value free samples

It is important to recognize that many offers around banking, legitimate trading opportunities and genuine job search and employment opportunities are not "Get Rich Quick" and should not be labeled as such.

### Added guidance — practical core test

Ask whether the visible content promotes unusually easy, fast, vague, suspicious, or highly remunerative money-making opportunities, including work-from-home schemes, lucrative secrets, MLM/pyramid-style structures, paid surveys, unrealistic trading/investment deals, or cash-reward apps covered by the manual. This is a shorthand for applying the official scope above, not a replacement for it.

## Official guidance

Look out for clues that can help you tell a legitimate opportunity from a potential fraud, e.g.:

Is it professionally or poorly edited? Are there a lot of typos?
Does the claim look too good to be true?
Do the promised profits look plausible or unrealistically high?
Does the ad or page accuse others of being scams?

In general, if an ad or page has certain characteristics of scammy / suspicious cases but you're not quite sure, assume it is positive for Get Rich Quick and rate it as positive.

### Added guidance — general practical positive signals for visible-evidence rating

- Unrealistically high earnings for the effort or work described (partly redundant with: official clue asks whether promised profits look plausible or unrealistically high)
- No explanation of what the business or opportunity actually is
- No explanation of how the advertiser profits
- Work-from-home / online / "be your own boss" framing (partly redundant with: official scope includes work from home offers)
- Defensive language, such as "this is NOT a scam" or "not get rich quick" (partly redundant with: official clue asks whether the ad or page accuses others of being scams)
- Secrets, hidden knowledge, or insider information being sold or given away (partly redundant with: official scope includes offers that promise to share lucrative business secrets)
- "Guaranteed" attached to an investment or earnings claim
- Conspiracy framing, such as "what banks don't want you to know"
- A "free" offer with no clear revenue model
- Suspicious URL text that suggests hidden-wealth or MLM schemes

### Added guidance — general practical negative signals for visible-evidence rating

- Household-name brand
- Clear one-sentence description of what the service does
- No earnings promise, or the reward is left unquantified
- Clear, obvious mechanism for how the advertiser makes money

### Added guidance — applying the Get Rich Quick uncertainty rule

When content is assessable but classification is difficult, first apply the official category-specific uncertainty rule. If the ad or page has scammy or suspicious Get Rich Quick characteristics but you are not quite sure, rate it positive for Get Rich Quick and leave a brief uncertainty comment. Do not use Unrateable for assessable ambiguity.

# Topics included in the Get Rich Quick category

## Official guidance

### (1) Content related to vaguely-defined opportunities to get rich quick.

This includes opportunities to make a lot of fast and easy money which are described in vague terms; sometimes they may require an initial downpayment or investment, sometimes they may promise free giveaways.

However it excludes clearly-described, genuine activities or opportunities which do not claim to lead to fast enrichment; it also excludes gambling, e.g. lotto.

Canonical Positive Topics:
✔️ promise of unrealistically high earnings, gains, or returns on investment
✔️ vague about the details of the opportunity
✔️ often working from home or online
✔️ sometimes self-describing as not scammy or not Get-Rich-Quick

Canonical Negative Topics:
❌ offer standard financial services, such as stock brokering, real estate services, money management, banking and credit card offers, and so on
❌ have a clear mechanism for making the advertiser money -- Get Rich Quick ads often present themselves as do-it-yourself offers and are rarely up front about how they expect to make money from the customer
❌ gambling, e.g. lotto

### Added guidance — practical positive signals for visible-evidence rating

- Promises high earnings with zero description of the opportunity (partly redundant with: official topic includes vague opportunities to make fast/easy money)
- "Be your own boss" / quit-your-job / escape-corporate-life framing
- Free giveaways paired with no clear revenue model
- Risk-free or guaranteed money-making language with no clear explanation of the underlying activity
- Upfront payment or downpayment before the opportunity is explained
- Shoddy copywriting or typos when paired with high earnings, vague opportunity, or work-from-home claims

### Added guidance — practical negative signals for visible-evidence rating

- Clearly-described, genuine activity with no fast-enrichment claim (partly redundant with: official topic excludes clearly-described genuine activities or opportunities)
- Standard financial service, banking, brokerage, real estate service, or money-management offer with no unrealistic return claim
- Clear mechanism for how the advertiser makes money
- Gambling or official lottery/sweepstakes context, rather than a Get Rich Quick opportunity

### (3) Content related to unrealistically remunerative business or job opportunities.

This includes job offers or business propositions that promise a huge amount of money or very high rewards.

It excludes genuine job offers and legitimate business opportunities that are advertised without emphatic remuneration promises.

### Added guidance — practical positive signals for visible-evidence rating

- Pay is unrealistically high for the type of work described (partly redundant with: official topic includes job offers or business propositions promising huge money or very high rewards)

- Specific, named role with a plausible market-rate salary
- Business opportunity clearly described in one sentence
- Genuine job offer or legitimate business opportunity without emphatic remuneration promises (partly redundant with: official topic excludes genuine job offers and legitimate business opportunities without emphatic remuneration promises)
- Business advice or business-service content that describes the activity clearly and does not promise unrealistically large earnings

### (9) Apps that offer cash rewards for the time users spend on the app.

This includes apps that promise cash just for engaging with the app, whether by playing a game, using a keyboard, or other vaguely defined activities.

### Added guidance — practical positive signals for visible-evidence rating

- It is a sweepstake or prize draw, where users are trying to win rather than being paid for activity

# Task analysis template

This section is the per-task analysis template the LLM should execute after reading the manual above. It structures the rating output.

## Task-analysis process

1. Identify what is visibly offered using only screenshot/OCR evidence.
2. Check whether the task is assessable at all. If not, rate Unrateable and briefly explain why.
3. If the task is assessable but classification is difficult, give the best Yes/No rating under the manual and add a brief uncertainty comment.
4. Do not use Unrateable as a fallback for ambiguous but assessable Get Rich Quick cases.
5. Sparse creatives can still be rateable if product name, visible URL, CTA, imagery, or branding provides enough evidence.
6. Apply the Get Rich Quick official uncertainty rule when choosing between Yes and No: if the ad or page has scammy or suspicious Get Rich Quick characteristics but you are not quite sure, assume it is positive and rate it as positive.
7. If an exception or outlier example seems relevant, apply it only to sufficiently similar cases and return to the main category definition otherwise.

## Per-task indicator extraction

For each task, list only indicators that actually apply to the visible content. Separate matching indicators by source:

- Official = directly present in original official guidance.
- Added = directly present in added guidance sections of this manual.
- Non-manual = useful practical indicators not explicitly present anywhere in this manual.

When listing indicators in the output table, prefix a non-official indicator with `~` if it is at least partly redundant with an Official indicator already listed for that same task.

- This applies to indicators in the **Added** and **Non-manual** rows.
- The `~` means: this indicator is still useful to surface, but it overlaps with an Official indicator already present and should not be read as fully independent extra evidence.
- Do not use `~` merely because an indicator is generally related to official guidance in the manual; use it only when the overlap is visible in the specific task’s extracted indicator set.

Use “None” in any indicator-table cell or Unrateable-indicator line where no matching indicator applies.

## Before-finalizing check

Before finalizing, check:

- Am I using Unrateable only because the task cannot be assessed at all?
- If I rated Yes, is there visible evidence that the content belongs to the Get Rich Quick category under the manual?
- If I rated No, am I ignoring strong visible evidence that the content belongs to the Get Rich Quick category?
- If the official suspicious/scammy uncertainty-bias rule applies, did I apply it correctly?
- If an exception seems relevant, is this task sufficiently similar to the canonical/example exception?
